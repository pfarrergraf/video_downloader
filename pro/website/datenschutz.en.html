2. General information on data processing

We process personal data only to the extent necessary to provide our services (the DownloadThat app and Pro licensing). Processing is carried out on the basis of the GDPR and the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG).

The app itself runs entirely on your device and does not transmit usage data or downloaded content to our servers. Data processing only occurs in connection with purchasing and validating a Pro license.

3. Data processing when purchasing a Pro license

3.1 Payment processing (Stripe)

When you purchase a Pro license, payment is processed via the service Stripe (Stripe Inc., 510 Townsend Street, San Francisco, CA 94103, USA). Stripe processes payment data (e.g. card details), your email address, and any other information required for payment processing.

We do not store complete payment data ourselves. Stripe may transmit the following information to us after a successful payment:

  • Stripe checkout session ID (to match the purchase)
  • Email address (if provided by Stripe)
  • License tier (monthly / yearly / lifetime)

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract).
More information on data processing by Stripe is available at: https://stripe.com/privacy .

3.2 License key database (Cloudflare D1)

After a successful payment, a license key is generated and stored in a database operated via Cloudflare D1 (Cloudflare, Inc., 101 Townsend St, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA). The following is stored:

  • A unique license key (UUID)
  • The Stripe checkout session ID
  • The license tier and activation timestamp
  • Email address (if provided by Stripe)

The data is stored for the duration of the license. For lifetime licenses, until deletion is requested by the licensee or the service is discontinued. Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract).
Cloudflare privacy policy: https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/ .

3.3 License validation in the app

When you enter a license key in the app, it is transmitted once to our license API endpoint (also hosted on Cloudflare Pages) for validation. Only the key itself is checked; no device data, IP addresses, or other information is permanently stored.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract).

3.4 Proof of consent to early contract performance

When purchasing a Pro license, you must expressly confirm before payment that contract performance should begin before the statutory withdrawal period ends (see Terms of Use §6.3). To document this declaration, the timestamp of your confirmation is stored in text form as part of the order data transmitted to Stripe (the checkout session's reference number). Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (legal obligation to provide evidence under § 356(5) of the German Civil Code (BGB)) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in securing evidence in the event of a dispute).

3.5 Refund requests

If you request a refund via the self-service function (Withdrawal & Refund), we process the email address and license key you enter in order to match your purchase with Stripe and to process the refund and license deactivation. Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) and (c) GDPR (contract processing and fulfillment of statutory withdrawal and refund obligations).

4. Hosting and technical infrastructure

This website is hosted via Cloudflare Pages. Cloudflare may temporarily store technical connection data (e.g. IP addresses) in its logs. This serves operational security and is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in the secure and error-free provision of the website). More information: Cloudflare privacy policy .

5. No cookies, no trackers

This website does not set its own cookies and does not use tracking or analytics services. Stripe may set cookies on its own pages as part of the payment process — these are outside our sphere of influence.

6. Data transfers to third countries

Stripe and Cloudflare are headquartered in the USA. Data transfer is based on EU standard contractual clauses (Art. 46(2)(c) GDPR) or the EU-US Data Privacy Framework (where applicable). More information can be found in the respective providers' privacy policies.
